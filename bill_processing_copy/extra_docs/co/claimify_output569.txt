
=== Division: Drinking water and wastewater infrastructure ===
--- Title: Clean water ---
Section: Clean water infrastructure resiliency and sustainability program(c) (c) Use of funds An eligible entity that receives a grant under the program shall use the grant funds for planning, designing, or constructing projects (on a system-wide or area-wide basis) that increase the resilience of a publicly owned treatment works to a natural hazard or cybersecurity vulnerabilities through— (1) (1) the conservation of water; (2) (2) the enhancement of water use efficiency; (3) (3) the enhancement of wastewater and stormwater management by increasing watershed preservation and protection, including through the use of— (A) (A) natural and engineered green infrastructure; and (B) (B) reclamation and reuse of wastewater and stormwater, such as aquifer recharge zones; (4) (4) the modification or relocation of an existing publicly owned treatment works, conveyance, or discharge system component that is at risk of being significantly impaired or damaged by a natural hazard; (5) (5) the development and implementation of projects to increase the resilience of publicly owned treatment works to a natural hazard or cybersecurity vulnerabilities, as applicable; or (6) (6) the enhancement of energy efficiency or the use and generation of recovered or renewable energy in the management, treatment, or conveyance of wastewater or stormwater..(1) (1) the conservation of water;.(2) (2) the enhancement of water use efficiency;.(3) (3) the enhancement of wastewater and stormwater management by increasing watershed preservation and protection, including through the use of— (A) (A) natural and engineered green infrastructure; Section: Small and medium publicly owned treatment works circuit rider program(a) (a) Establishment Subject to the availability of appropriations, not later than 180 days after the date of enactment of this section, the Administrator shall establish a circuit rider program (referred to in this section as the circuit rider program ) under which the Administrator shall award grants to qualified nonprofit entities, as determined by the Administrator, to provide assistance to owners and operators of small and medium publicly owned treatment works to carry out the activities described in section 602(b)(13)..(b) (b) Limitation A grant provided under the circuit rider program shall be in an amount that is not more than $75,000..(c) (c) Prioritization In selecting recipients of grants under the circuit rider program, the Administrator shall give priority to qualified nonprofit entities, as determined by the Administrator, that would serve a community that— (1) (1) has a history, for not less than the 10 years prior to the award of the grant, of unresolved wastewater issues, stormwater issues, or a combination of wastewater and stormwater issues; (2) (2) is considered financially distressed; (3) (3) faces the cumulative burden of stormwater and wastewater overflow issues; or (4) (4) has previously failed to access Federal technical assistance due to cost-sharing requirements..
