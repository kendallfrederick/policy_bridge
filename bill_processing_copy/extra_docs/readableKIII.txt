
=== Division K: Minority business development ===
--- Title III: Rural minority business center program ---

(100301.) Definitions: 

(1) (1) Appropriate congressional committees The term appropriate congressional committees means— (A) (A) the Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation of the Senate; and (B) (B) the Committee on Financial Services of the House of Representatives..(A) (A) the Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation of the Senate; and.(B) (B) the Committee on Financial Services of the House of Representatives..(2) (2) Eligible entity The term eligible entity means— (A) (A) a minority-serving institution; or (B) (B) a consortium of institutions of higher education that is led by a minority-serving institution..(A) (A) a minority-serving institution; or.(B) (B) a consortium of institutions of higher education that is led by a minority-serving institution..(3) (3) MBDA Rural Business Center The term MBDA Rural Business Center means an MBDA Business Center that provides technical business assistance to minority business enterprises located in rural areas..(4) (4) MBDA Rural Business Center agreement The term MBDA Rural Business Center agreement means an MBDA Business Center agreement that establishes the terms by which the recipient of the Federal assistance award that is the subject of the agreement shall operate an MBDA Rural Business Center..(5) (5) Minority-serving institution The term minority-serving institution means an institution described in any of paragraphs (1) through (7) of section 371(a) of the Higher Education Act of 1965 ( 20 U.S.C. 1067q(a) )..(6) (6) Rural area The term rural area has the meaning given the term in section 343(a) of the Consolidated Farm and Rural Development Act ( 7 U.S.C. 1991(a) )..(7) (7) Rural minority business enterprise The term rural minority business enterprise means a minority business enterprise located in a rural area..

(100303.) Report to Congress: 

(1) (1) a summary of the efforts of the Under Secretary to provide services to minority business enterprises located in States that lack an MBDA Business Center, as of the date of enactment of this Act, and especially in those States that have significant minority populations; and.(2) (2) recommendations for extending the outreach of the Agency to underserved areas..

(100304.) Study and report: 

(a) (a) In general The Under Secretary, in coordination with relevant leadership of the Agency and relevant individuals outside of the Department of Commerce, shall conduct a study that addresses the ways in which minority business enterprises can meet gaps in the supply chain of the United States, with a particular focus on the supply chain of advanced manufacturing and essential goods and services..(b) (b) Report Not later than 1 year after the date of enactment of this Act, the Under Secretary shall submit to the appropriate congressional committees a report that includes the results of the study conducted under subsection (a), which shall include recommendations regarding the ways in which minority business enterprises can meet gaps in the supply chain of the United States..
