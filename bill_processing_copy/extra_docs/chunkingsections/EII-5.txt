

(223.) Clean water infrastructure resiliency and sustainability program: 

(a) (a) Definitions In this section: (1) (1) Eligible entity The term eligible entity means— (A) (A) a municipality; or (B) (B) an intermunicipal, interstate, or State agency. (2) (2) Natural hazard The term natural hazard means a hazard caused by natural forces, including extreme weather events, sea-level rise, and extreme drought conditions. (3) (3) Program The term program means the clean water infrastructure resilience and sustainability program established under subsection (b)..(1) (1) Eligible entity The term eligible entity means— (A) (A) a municipality; (2) (2) the enhancement of water use efficiency; (3) (3) the enhancement of wastewater and stormwater management by increasing watershed preservation and protection, including through the use of— (A) (A) natural and engineered green infrastructure; and (B) (B) reclamation and reuse of wastewater and stormwater, such as aquifer recharge zones; (4) (4) the modification or relocation of an existing publicly owned treatment works, conveyance, or discharge system component that is at risk of being significantly impaired or damaged by a natural hazard; (5) (5) the development and implementation of projects to increase the resilience of publicly owned treatment works to a natural hazard or cybersecurity vulnerabilities, as applicable; or (6) (6) the enhancement of energy efficiency or the use and generation of recovered or renewable energy in the management, treatment, or conveyance of wastewater or stormwater..(1) (1) the conservation of water;.(2) (2) the enhancement of water use efficiency;.(3) (3) the enhancement of wastewater and stormwater management by increasing watershed preservation and protection, including through the use of— (A) (A) natural and engineered green infrastructure; (2) (2) an identification of the natural hazard risk of the area where the proposed project is to be located or potential cybersecurity vulnerability, as applicable, to be addressed by the proposed project; (3) (3) documentation prepared by a Federal, State, regional, or local government agency of the natural hazard risk of the area where the proposed project is to be located or potential cybersecurity vulnerability, as applicable, of the area where the proposed project is to be located; (4) (4) a description of any recent natural hazard risk of the area where the proposed project is to be located or potential cybersecurity vulnerabilities that have affected the publicly owned treatment works; (5) (5) a description of how the proposed project would improve the performance of the publicly owned treatment works under an anticipated natural hazard or natural hazard risk of the area where the proposed project is to be located or a potential cybersecurity vulnerability, as applicable;
